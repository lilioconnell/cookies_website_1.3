In 2013, I visited New York, and on that trip took a stop at the world famous

Levain Bakery.

I had a chocolate chip cookie and to this day, it has not been topped by any other.

I am going to be attempting to recreate these cookies by using a recipe I found online claiming to taste almost the exact same. I am excited to see if they will taste the same as the original and the differences between the two. Here you will find the step by step method and results!

RECIPE

The ingredients in this recipe are:

  • 115 grams unsalted butter
  • 190 grams brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract, 1 large egg
  • 245 grams plain flour or all purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • Pinch of salt, optional
  • 225 grams dark chocolate
  • *optional* 50 grams of walnuts

Feel free to follow along at home! I chose to opt out of using walnuts as I am not a fan of their flavour

FINISHED COOKIES AND GALLERY

These cookies turned out delicious, although they lacked the thickness of the ones from Levain bakery. This could be due to me choosing not to use walnuts and other small factors that contribute to differences between bakeries, ovens, and ingredients. They were still very yummy and will be enjoyed by myself and my coaches! :)
